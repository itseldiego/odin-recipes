Tacos al estilo Tijuana

"Tacos al estilo de Tijuana" (TJ-style tacos) are know for their bold flavors, fresh ingredients and street style authenticity. Today, we will learn to re-create Tacos de Adobada(Al Pastor) Tacos. Here is the recipe that will blow your taste buds away:

Ingredients:

Marinating the meat (Adobada):

  • 2 lbs pork shoulder or pork loin, sliced thin
  • 2 dried guajillo chilies (remove seeds, soak in hot water)
  • 2 dried ancho chilies (remove seeds, soak in hot water)
  • 2 dried árbol chilies (optional for spice)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons achiote paste (adds color and flavor, found in Latin stores)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon oil (for cooking)

For the Tacos

  • 12 small corn tortillas (street-style size)
  • 1/2 cup chopped white onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 cup grilled pineapple chunks (adds sweetness)
  • Lime wedges

Salsa Roja (Red Sauce):

p.s one can use store bought sauce but this homemade recipe will bring the tacos to new heights ;)

  • 3 Roma tomatoes, roasted or charred
  • 2 dried guajillo chilies (remove seeds, soak in hot water)
  • 1 dried árbol chili (optional for spice)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make the Marinade

    2. -Soak the dried chilies in hot water for about 10 minutes to soften them.

    -Blend the chilies, garlic, orange juice, pineapple juice, vinegar, achiote paste, and spices until smooth.

    -Pour the marinade over the pork, making sure every piece is coated. Cover and let it marinate for at least 4 hours (overnight is best).

  2. Make the Salsa Roja

    3. Roast the Roma tomatoes on a dry pan until the skin blackens. Blend the roasted tomatoes with the softened guajillo and árbol chilies, garlic, water, and salt until smooth.

  3. Cook the Pork

    4. Grill Method:

    Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

    Cook the pork slices for 3-4 minutes per side until slightly charred.

    Stovetop Method:

    Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat.

    Cook the pork slices in batches, making sure they get a nice sear.

    Once the pork is done, let it rest for 5 minutes, then chop it into small pieces.

  4. Assemble the Tacos:
    1. Warm the tortillas on a dry pan until they are soft and slightly crispy.
    2. Add some chopped pork to each tortilla.
    3. Top with chopped onion, cilantro, and grilled pineapple.
    4. Drizzle with salsa roja and squeeze fresh lime juice on top.
